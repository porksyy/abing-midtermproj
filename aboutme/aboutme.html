CHILDHOOD YEARS

            My name is John Reno Abing and I was born in Parañaque, Metro Manila.1 I only have 1 sibling, my older sister and we have a pretty big age gap; 12 years.2The earliest memory that I can recall from my childhood is when I was 6 years old, I was playing with my friends on our school playground and we were just enjoying our toys and goofing around.3 I was attending a school near from where we live which was convenient.4 The school was called YAIS and it is located in Las Piñas.5My baon was always prepared by my mother and I enjoyed it every day.6 In addition to my baon, I would also buy Stick-O from the school cafeteria.7 Every day, it was always the same; 20 pesos worth of Stick-O, which was a lot by then since I was only a child.8 It got to the point that the cafeteria lady already prepared the Stick-O beforehand so that I would just give her the 20 pesos when I entered the cafeteria.9 They got so used to my routine and would always be shocked if I did not buy any Stick-O for the day.10 The only downside was that the cafeteria in our building did not have Stick-O.11 So, I had to go out every day and there was always a guard or staff that would accompany me since they also knew my routine.12 I attended Young Achievers International school, or YAIS, for 5 years from Kinder up to Grade 3.13

              I then transferred to SAS, for the remainder of my elementary days.14 This was also the start of my eyes already getting blurry so I had to get glasses at a young age of 11 years old.15 It was difficult at the time since I had to get used to wearing glasses everyday, or else I would have a difficult time at school.16 I remember being excited when I was in 6th grade since the year after, I would be a highschool student.17 I just liked the tone of being a highschool student and it made me think that I would be mature that time.18 We had a "gratitude night" nearing the end of my grade 6 year.19 It was similar to a prom and we had to practice dances weeks before the event itself.20 I remember being excited in the morning of the event since it was going to be my first time wearing a tuxedo and I was thinking to myself that I was going to be so cool and look like an adult21.

We still meet up from time to time whenever we have free time. 5 It was also my first grasp at relationships and it taught me a lot;more than what I needed to know.6We were both a lesson to each other and I believe it made us into a better person.7I also made a lot of memories which I can remember vividly.8One notable memory was that when we were finishing our clearance, everyone was scrambling around trying to find the teachers so that they can sign their clearance.9 There was a point where almost everyone was in the room at the time and we were generally tired since the teachers were also busy with other students. 10Everyone was just chilling at our room and my classmate played a song over the speaker.11 It was a playlist of 2000s R&B songs and pretty much everyone knew the lyrics to the songs so we were all just singing along and just having a good time in general.12

             After a while, everyone got back to completing their clearance.13 Initially, I was tired since I kept walking all day but when my classmate played those songs, I got an energy recharge and I was feeling active again.14 I also met a plenty of teachers that would change how I viewed certain subjects.15 One particular teacher was Ms. Zeth, my research teacher at the time.16 Before she was my teacher, I hated doing research since it was too time consuming and I just did not like it.17 When Ms. Zeth became my teacher, she was so passionate in teaching research that it made me listen attentively to her class.18 It got to the point where I was actually excited on certain days because we would have our research subject on that day.19 From then on, I never really dreaded research that much.20
